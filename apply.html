Expand your skills at our free, intensive learn-to-code bootcamp

Your future is on the Web

Are you curious about software? Have you ever experimented with HTML or JavaScript, or taken an online course? We can help you learn more. Our free, intensive learn-to-code bootcamp includes training in the "full stack" of web development. That means everything from HTML and CSS to JavaScript, Node, Express and more, plus Agile, effective communication, and job readiness.

Classes start January 29, 2019


To apply to our program, you must have a high school diploma or GED, and be legal to work in the United States through December 2020. But NO coding experience is required, and there is NO maximum age. Classes will be held Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, 6:30pm-9pm, and Saturdays 9am-2pm. Classes run from January 29 through June 22, 2019, with an orientation session for admitted applicants on January 22.

Bonus recruiting event announced

Want to learn more before you apply? Join us for a Coding & Career Event at Lena Park Community Development on Thursday, Jan. 10, 2019 from 6:30pm-8:30pm.

Ready to apply?

Attending an event is recommended, but NOT required to apply. If you can't make it, or you're already interested in the program, send in your application now. We can fill in all the details during the application process, and there is no commitment until the bootcamp starts. We are looking forward to meeting you!


CodeSquad is a nonprofit organization that trains talented adults from low-income families to become full-stack developers and launch a new career.
