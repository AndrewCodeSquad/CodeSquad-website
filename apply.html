Your future is on the Web

Are you ready for a career change?

Do you like to solve puzzles? Do you wonder how websites and apps are made? Our free, intensive learn-to-code bootcamp can teach you the "full stack" of web development. That includes everything from HTML and CSS to JavaScript, Node, Express, Mongo and more, plus effective communication and job readiness.

Classes start in January


To apply to our program, you must have a high school diploma (or equivalent), and be legal to work in the United States for at least one year after graduation. But NO coding experience is required, and there is NO maximum age. Classes will be held Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, 6:30pm-9pm, and Saturdays 9am-2pm. Classes run from January through June.

What are the events like?

At CodeSquad recruiting events, students jump right into coding by learning HTML in a team challenge. First, we teach you enough HTML to build a simple web page for a small company, and volunteers can help if you get stuck. Next, each team presents its web page and receives informal feedback. The event concludes with a career panel featuring volunteers from the software industry and/or CodeSquad graduates, who talk about how they got started in technology and answer questions.

Ready to apply?

Attending an event is recommended, but NOT required to apply. If you can't make it, or you're already interested in the program, send in your application now. We can fill in all the details during the application process, and there is no commitment until the bootcamp starts. We are looking forward to meeting you!


CodeSquad is a nonprofit organization that trains talented adults from low-income families to become full-stack developers and launch a new career.
