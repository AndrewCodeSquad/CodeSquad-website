Help bridge the Kenmore Square - Dudley Square divide

CodeSquad was born from the realization that Boston's booming tech economy was leaving behind the city's urban core. There is a stark contrast in unemployment rates, salaries and demographics the city's high-tech districts and neighborhoods like Dorchester, Roxbury, Mattapan, Hyde Park and Jamaica Plain. We sometimes think of this as the distance between Dudley Square and Kenmore Square. CodeSquad seeks to build a bridge between these two communities, bringing the benefits of software careers (high pay, flexible hours, creative work, long-term growth) to Boston's urban neighborhoods.

Classroom projects and homework are designed to mimic the experience of team-based software development. Class communication takes place largely on Slack, assignments are submitted via GitHub, and resources are shared through our learning management system.

Your donation is invested in empowering Boston's underrepresented communities of color to compete for careers in the tech industry. Your investment provides for upward mobility, economic independence and diversity in Boston's expanding job market.

CodeSquad is a non-profit organization that trains talented adults from Boston's urban core to become full-stack web developers and launch a new career. Students are trained in web design and server-side development, as well as Agile methodology, effective communication and teamwork. Following their training, CodeSquad works to place graduates in internships and full-time technology roles in greater Boston.
