the Kenmore Square - Dudley Square Divide

CodeSquad was born from the realization that Boston’s booming tech economy was leaving behind the city’s local communities of color. There is a stark contrast in unemployment rate, average salary and job security between Greater Boston's high tech districts and neighborhoods like Dorchester, Roxbury, Mattapan, Hyde Park, East Boston and Jamaica Plain. We call this the Dudley Square - Kenmore Square Divide to illustrate the short 2.5 mile distance between the two neighborhoods, and the large difference in the standard of living amongst their residents. CodeSquad seeks to build a bridge of upward mobility and economic independence by creating a pipeline of local, diverse talent to fully participate in Boston's expanding job market.

CodeSquad is a non-profit 501(c)3 organization that trains Boston's untapped, talented, adult, local communities of color to become full-stack web developers and launch new careers. Students are trained in in-demand skills of web design (front-end), server-side (back-end) development, Agile methodology, communication, peer programming and teamwork. CodeSquad works to place graduates in internships and full-time technology roles.

CodeSquad is a nonprofit organization that trains talented adults from Boston's underrepresented, local communities of color to become full-stack developers and launch a new career in tech.
