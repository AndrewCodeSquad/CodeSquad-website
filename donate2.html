We need your help to bridge the
Kendall Square - Dudley Square Divide

CodeSquad was born from the realization that Boston's booming tech economy was leaving behind the city's local communities of color. High tech districts like Kendall Square, Cambridge have low unemployment, high average salaries and excellent job security, while the picture is very different in neighborhoods like Dorchester, Roxbury, Mattapan, Hyde Park, East Boston and Jamaica Plain. We call this the Dudley to Kendall Divide. CodeSquad seeks to build a bridge of upward mobility from Dudley to Kendall, by creating a pipeline of local, diverse talent for Boston's expanding technology job market.
Invest in people. Donate to CodeSquad.

Students are trained in in-demand skills of web design (front-end), server-side (back-end) development, Agile methodology, communication, peer programming and teamwork. CodeSquad works to place graduates in internships and full-time technology roles.

CodeSquad is a nonprofit organization that trains talented adults from Boston's underrepresented, local communities of color to become full-stack developers and launch a new career in tech.
