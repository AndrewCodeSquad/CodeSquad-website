CodeSquad is excited to welcome our ninth cohort of aspiring software developers as they begin their journey into technology careers

 

Ramlose grant provides laptops for students


Thanks to a grant from the George A. Ramlose Foundation, students Jaiden Wilson and Miguel Melendez received new laptops for the 2026 Bootcamp. Reliable technology is crucial to our students’ success. Thank you to the Ramlose Foundation for empowering the next generation of tech talent!

 

What are they learning?


Our 2026 Cohort is rapidly gaining the technical skills of modern web development. They are learning to write JavaScript functions and loops and to manipulate the Document Object Model, a hierarchical tree of components that make up every web page.

 


First Pathways website goes live

The Pathways Project recruits CodeSquad grads to build low-cost websites and apps for small businesses and nonprofits, providing paid opportunities to build their portfolios. CodeSquad grads have already completed one website, for 365Dad, while two are under development and six are in the scoping phase.


“I was so nervous revealing the website to the client. But he was so happy, he said he wanted to jump up and down. Together, we were making a difference. That thought made my heart happy.”


- Nicole Payne, 2024 Cohort Graduate, discussing her Pathways Project
